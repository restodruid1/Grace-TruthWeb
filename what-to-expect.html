The Meeting

What Happens During a Service

Our Sunday Morning Worship is led by members of the congregation in what we call the "priesthood of all believers." Men lead the gathering through hymns, prayers, and readings from Scripture, with an emphasis on direct, unmediated access to God for every worshipper.

During the morning worship service, we observe communion (the Lord's Supper) together, sharing bread and wine as symbols of Christ's body and blood. This is a meaningful and regular part of our worship. We ask first-time visitors to observe rather than participate in communion, so we can get to know one another before sharing the table together.

How to Dress

Dress & Appearance

We simply ask that all attendees dress respectfully and modestly, with the focus kept on worship and Jesus Christ rather than appearance.

You will notice that women in our fellowship cover their heads with scarves, hats, or similar accessories during meetings. This practice reflects our understanding of biblical teaching on spiritual authority and submission to Christ's headship, as described in 1 Corinthians 11. We hold this as a conviction, and we welcome visitors to come as they are without any pressure to conform right away.

If you have questions about any of our practices, we are always happy to talk. Our goal is transparency, not formality.
